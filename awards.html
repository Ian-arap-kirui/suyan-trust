Global Recognition

FAO Achievements Award 2022

Honored in Rome, Italy during World Food Day celebrations

The Kirisia Community Forest Association (K-CFA) was recognized on the global stage for its outstanding community-led efforts in restoring the Kirisia Forest, a critical water tower in Samburu, Kenya. Presented by the FAO Director-General QU Dongyu during the World Food Day celebrations in Rome, this award honored the voluntary relocation of over 10,000 people from the forest, the planting of 150,000+ trees, and the creation of sustainable livelihoods through beekeeping, eco-tourism, and agroforestry.

This milestone positioned KCFA as a global model for peaceful and sustainable forest conservation, biodiversity protection, and community resilience.

Why is Kirisia Forest important?

Kirisia Forest is a water tower, wildlife refuge, and cultural heritage site for Samburu County. It sustains biodiversity and provides essential ecosystem services for surrounding communities.

How can I support conservation efforts?

You can contribute by volunteering, spreading awareness, or partnering with organizations working to restore Kirisia. Every action helps protect this ecosystem.

Can I visit Kirisia Forest?

Yes! Kirisia is accessible via Maralal. Visitors can enjoy eco-tourism, birdwatching, and cultural experiences guided by local communities.

Who manages the forest?

The forest is co-managed by the Kenya Forest Service (KFS) and the Kirisia Community Forest Association (CFA), with support from local and international partners.
