Overview

Kirisia Community Forest Association (K-CFA) is dedicated to empowering communities while safeguarding the Kirisia Forest. Many households in Samburu rely on forest resources for survival, creating a pressing need for alternative livelihoods that protect the environment and uplift families. Through community-driven projects, training, and partnerships, K-CFA is enabling residents to transition to eco-friendly sources of income while reducing pressure on the forest.

Key Initiatives
  • ✅ Beekeeping & honey production – supporting over 550 hives to boost local incomes 🐝
  • ✅ Tree nurseries & agroforestry – four central and seven village nurseries supplying seedlings 🌱
  • ✅ Poultry & kitchen gardens – enhancing household food security 🐔
  • ✅ Eco-tourism & cultural tourism – including the Tamiyoi Camp to promote conservation-linked livelihoods ⛺
  • ✅ Alternative businesses – providing microfinance and training for former charcoal burners 💰
  • ✅ Education & awareness – training community members on sustainable resource use 📚
Impact
  • 🌿 600,000+ KES disbursed to support eco-friendly community businesses.
  • 🐝 Hundreds of beekeeping hives producing honey for local and regional markets.
  • 🌳 Tree nurseries producing seedlings for reforestation and household planting.
  • 👩‍👩‍👧 Dozens of households adopting kitchen gardens and poultry for food and income security.
  • 🌍 Reduced dependence on unsustainable forest products, protecting Kirisia Forest.
"Empowering the community means protecting the forest — and protecting the forest means securing the community’s future." 🌿

Partner With Us

We invite conservation groups, development agencies, and supporters to partner with us in advancing both community livelihoods and forest protection. Together, we can scale up these initiatives and create lasting impact in Samburu.

By working together, we can:

  • ✅ Expand reforestation efforts and tree planting programs.
  • ✅ Strengthen community rangers and forest patrols.
  • ✅ Develop sustainable eco-tourism opportunities like Tamiyoi Camp.
  • ✅ Provide resources and training for eco-friendly community enterprises.

K-CFA works in partnership with organizations such as the Kenya Forest Service (KFS), FAO, Suyian Trust, ACTED Kenya, and Samburu County Government. 🌍 Join us in empowering communities while conserving the Kirisia Forest.
